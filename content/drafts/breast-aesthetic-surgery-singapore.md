# Breast Aesthetic Surgery in Singapore

Breast aesthetic surgery refers to a range of procedures that assess and refine breast size, shape, symmetry, position and proportion. It may include breast augmentation, breast reduction, breast lift, implant revision, asymmetry correction or a combination of approaches.

The aim is not to create a standard breast shape. A well-planned operation should account for the patient’s anatomy, breast tissue quality, skin envelope, chest wall shape, nipple position, previous surgery, lifestyle, recovery needs and personal goals. The most appropriate plan differs between individuals.

This page provides general information and should not replace consultation with a qualified medical practitioner. Suitability, risks, recovery and outcomes vary between individuals.

## What is breast aesthetic surgery?

Breast aesthetic surgery includes operations that change breast volume, contour, position or balance. Some patients seek surgery because they feel their breasts are too small, too large, droopy, asymmetric, changed after pregnancy or weight loss, or affected by previous implants or prior surgery.

Depending on assessment, surgery may involve:

- increasing breast volume with implants or selected fat grafting
- reducing breast volume and weight
- lifting the breast by reshaping tissue and repositioning the nipple-areola complex
- improving breast asymmetry where possible
- revising or replacing previous breast implants
- addressing implant-related concerns such as malposition, capsular contracture, rippling or size mismatch
- combining breast surgery with body-contouring procedures in selected patients

The surgical plan should be individualised. A size or shape that suits one person may not suit another person’s body frame, tissue quality or long-term priorities.

## Who may consider breast aesthetic surgery?

Breast aesthetic surgery may be considered by patients who are concerned about:

- small breast volume or loss of volume after pregnancy, breastfeeding or weight change
- breast drooping or nipple position changes
- heavy breasts causing discomfort, skin irritation, exercise limitation or difficulty with clothing
- breast asymmetry
- tuberous or constricted breast shape
- previous breast implants requiring review or revision
- implant-related symptoms or visible changes
- changes after previous breast surgery

Some concerns are mainly aesthetic. Others may have a functional component, particularly when heavy breasts contribute to neck, shoulder or back discomfort, skin irritation or exercise difficulty. A consultation is needed to assess anatomy, symptoms, goals and whether surgery may be appropriate.

## Breast augmentation

Breast augmentation aims to increase breast volume and refine breast proportion. It is most commonly performed with breast implants, although fat grafting may be discussed in selected patients where appropriate.

Planning breast augmentation involves more than choosing an implant size. Important considerations include:

- chest width and breast base diameter
- existing breast tissue and skin envelope
- breast asymmetry
- nipple position
- implant type, shape and profile
- implant pocket position
- incision location
- lifestyle, exercise and recovery needs
- long-term implant monitoring and possible future revision

Implants can increase breast volume, but they cannot make both breasts perfectly identical or stop natural breast changes over time. Pregnancy, breastfeeding, ageing, weight change and tissue quality may affect the breast appearance after surgery.

### Implant pocket and incision planning

Breast implants may be placed in different tissue planes depending on anatomy and surgical goals. The incision may be planned in the inframammary fold, around the areola or in another selected location depending on the patient and surgeon’s assessment.

Each option has trade-offs. The discussion should include scar position, implant coverage, recovery, implant movement, visibility, rippling risk and how future revision or implant exchange may be managed if needed.

### Fat grafting for breast volume

Fat grafting uses fat taken from another part of the body and transferred to the breast. It may be considered for selected patients who want modest volume change, contour refinement or soft-tissue improvement. It is not a substitute for implants when a larger volume increase is desired.

Fat grafting has its own limitations. Some transferred fat may not survive, more than one session may be needed, and the amount of achievable volume change depends on donor fat availability, breast tissue capacity and healing response.

## Breast reduction

Breast reduction, or reduction mammoplasty, reduces breast size and weight while reshaping the breast and repositioning the nipple-areola complex. It may be considered for patients with heavy breasts, discomfort, skin irritation, exercise difficulty, posture-related symptoms or difficulty finding well-fitting clothing.

The operation usually involves removal of breast tissue and skin, breast reshaping and scars around the areola, vertically down the breast and sometimes along the inframammary fold. The exact scar pattern depends on breast size, droop, tissue quality and the amount of reduction needed.

Breast reduction can reduce breast weight and improve proportion in appropriately selected patients, but outcomes vary. Scars, changes in nipple sensation, breastfeeding considerations, asymmetry and the possibility of future changes should be discussed carefully.

## Breast lift / mastopexy

A breast lift, or mastopexy, reshapes and lifts the breast without primarily aiming to increase volume. It may be considered when the breast has dropped, the nipple sits low, or the skin envelope has stretched after pregnancy, breastfeeding, weight change or ageing.

A breast lift may involve:

- repositioning the nipple-areola complex
- reshaping breast tissue
- removing selected excess skin
- improving the breast envelope
- adjusting asymmetry where possible

A lift does not usually create the same volume increase as an implant. Some patients may consider mastopexy alone; others may need augmentation-mastopexy if they want both lift and volume enhancement. Combining lift and implant surgery can be more complex and may carry different risks compared with either operation alone.

## Augmentation-mastopexy

Augmentation-mastopexy combines breast implant augmentation with a breast lift. It may be considered when a patient wants both increased volume and correction of breast droop.

This combined operation requires careful planning because the goals of increasing volume and tightening the skin envelope can place competing demands on the breast tissue. Potential issues include wound healing problems, scar widening, implant malposition, recurrent droop, asymmetry and need for revision surgery.

In some patients, a staged approach may be safer or more predictable than doing everything in one operation. The decision depends on anatomy, degree of droop, tissue quality, implant size goals and risk tolerance.

## Breast implant revision

Breast implant revision may be considered when a patient has concerns after previous breast augmentation or reconstruction. Reasons may include implant malposition, capsular contracture, rupture, rippling, visible implant edges, asymmetry, size concerns, discomfort or changes after pregnancy, ageing or weight change.

Revision surgery can be more complex than first-time breast augmentation because scar tissue, capsule quality, previous implant pocket, skin stretch and tissue thinning may affect the available options. Management may involve implant exchange, capsule surgery, pocket adjustment, fat grafting, implant removal, lift, or a combination of approaches.

Patients considering implant revision should understand that revision surgery may improve selected concerns, but it cannot always fully correct all shape, skin or tissue limitations.

## Breast asymmetry correction

Most patients naturally have some breast asymmetry. Surgery may be considered when asymmetry is significant or troubling. Depending on the situation, correction may involve augmentation, reduction, lift, fat grafting, implant revision or different procedures on each side.

The goal is usually to improve balance rather than create perfect symmetry. Differences in chest wall shape, breast footprint, nipple position, skin quality and healing response may remain after surgery.

## What happens during consultation?

A breast aesthetic surgery consultation may include:

- discussion of the patient’s concerns and goals
- assessment of breast size, shape, skin quality and asymmetry
- review of nipple position and breast droop
- chest wall and body proportion assessment
- review of previous breast surgery, implants, scars or complications
- discussion of implant, reduction, lift, fat grafting or revision options where appropriate
- explanation of scars, recovery, risks and possible revision needs
- review of medical history, medications, smoking status and breast screening history
- discussion of realistic expectations and limitations

Patients may be asked about their preferred breast size, comfort with scars, lifestyle, exercise, future pregnancy plans, tolerance for implants or future revision, and the trade-offs they are willing to accept.

## Breast screening and medical considerations

Breast symptoms such as a new lump, nipple discharge, unexplained skin change or sudden breast change should be medically assessed before aesthetic surgery is considered. Patients may also need age-appropriate breast screening or imaging depending on history, symptoms and clinical findings.

Breast aesthetic surgery should be planned with patient safety in mind. The consultation should include discussion of relevant medical conditions, medications, smoking, previous breast disease, family history and whether additional assessment is needed before surgery.

## Recovery after breast aesthetic surgery

Recovery depends on the procedure performed, the extent of surgery and the patient’s healing response. Breast augmentation, breast reduction, breast lift and implant revision have different recovery patterns.

Patients may need:

- wound care and follow-up appointments
- temporary surgical bra or support garment use
- temporary restriction of heavy lifting, strenuous exercise and upper-body activity
- time away from work depending on the operation and job demands
- monitoring for swelling, bruising, bleeding, infection or wound issues
- gradual return to exercise based on surgical advice

Swelling, bruising, tightness and changes in sensation are common during early recovery. Scars continue to mature over months and may remain visible. Final breast shape may continue to evolve as swelling settles and tissues relax.

## Risks and limitations

All surgery carries risks. Potential risks of breast aesthetic surgery may include:

- bleeding or haematoma
- infection
- wound healing problems
- scarring or thickened scars
- asymmetry
- changes in nipple or breast sensation
- delayed healing or skin loss
- fat necrosis or firm areas
- seroma or fluid collection
- breast shape, size or position differing from expectations
- recurrent droop or long-term changes with ageing, pregnancy or weight change
- difficulty or inability to breastfeed after some procedures
- need for revision surgery
- anaesthetic risks

Additional implant-related risks may include:

- capsular contracture
- implant rupture or leakage
- implant malposition or rotation
- rippling or visible implant edges
- implant-related discomfort
- need for future implant exchange, removal or revision
- rare implant-associated conditions that should be discussed during consultation

Breast aesthetic surgery can refine breast size, shape or proportion, but it cannot create perfect symmetry or prevent future changes. Outcomes depend on anatomy, tissue quality, healing response, surgical planning and long-term factors.

## Frequently asked questions

### Is breast augmentation suitable for everyone?

No. Suitability depends on breast anatomy, skin quality, health, goals, expectations and willingness to accept implant-related maintenance or future revision. Some patients may be better suited to fat grafting, breast lift, staged surgery or no surgery.

### Is breast reduction only cosmetic?

Not always. Breast reduction may have both aesthetic and functional components, especially when heavy breasts contribute to discomfort, skin irritation, exercise limitation or difficulty with clothing. Assessment is needed to determine the relevant issues for each patient.

### Will a breast lift add volume?

A breast lift reshapes and lifts the breast but does not primarily add volume. Some patients feel the breast looks fuller because the tissue is repositioned, but patients who want a clear volume increase may need to discuss implants or fat grafting.

### Can implants last forever?

Breast implants should not be considered lifetime devices. Some patients may need implant exchange, removal or revision in the future because of implant rupture, capsular contracture, malposition, ageing-related changes or personal preference.

### Can breast surgery correct asymmetry completely?

Surgery may improve selected asymmetry concerns, but perfect symmetry is not realistic. Differences in chest wall shape, breast footprint, nipple position, tissue quality and healing response may remain.

### When should breast symptoms be checked before aesthetic surgery?

Patients should seek medical assessment for symptoms such as a new breast lump, nipple discharge, unexplained skin change, sudden breast change, persistent pain or other concerning findings before proceeding with aesthetic breast surgery.
